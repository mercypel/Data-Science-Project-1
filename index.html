Product Sales & Weather Analysis

Predictive Modelling using R & SQL

📌 Project Overview

This project explores how weather conditions influence retail product sales. Using R and a SQLite database, the analysis compares linear regression and decision tree models across three products to understand customer behaviour and support demand forecasting.

🔧 Methodology

  • SQL joins to merge sales + weather data
  • Date formatting and missing value handling
  • Outlier winsorisation (99.5th percentile)
  • Feature engineering: weekend & rain indicators
  • Models: Linear Regression & Decision Tree
  • Train/Test/Validation split: 60/20/20

📈 Model Performance

  • Product 1: Linear RMSE 27.46 (best)
  • Product 2: Linear RMSE 24.52 (best)
  • Product 3: Linear RMSE 24.05 (best)
  • Weather explains only a small portion of sales (low R²)
  • Weekend effect is the strongest predictor

💡 Key Insights

  • Product 1 performs better on cool, dry days
  • Product 2 is barely affected by weather
  • Product 3 is slightly sensitive to rain
  • Weekends consistently increase sales

🚀 Business Recommendations

  • Increase Product 1 stock on cool, dry days
  • Increase Product 2 stock on weekends
  • Increase Product 3 stock before weekends
  • Reduce stock for rainy days (Products 1 & 3)
